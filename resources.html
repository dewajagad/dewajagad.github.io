Books Worth Reading

📖

The Little Book of Common Sense Investing

John Bogle's classic argument for index fund investing. Clear, compelling, and shorter than you'd expect. A great first book.

Book
📖

A Random Walk Down Wall Street

Burton Malkiel's influential case for efficient markets and passive investing. Updated regularly. A thorough read for the serious beginner.

Book
📖

The Psychology of Money

Morgan Housel on the behavioral and emotional side of money decisions. Probably the most readable investing book published in the last decade.

Book
📖

The Intelligent Investor

Benjamin Graham's foundational text on value investing. Dense in places, but worth it. Warren Buffett called it "the best book on investing ever written."

Book
📖

One Up On Wall Street

Peter Lynch's approachable guide to stock picking based on everyday observation. Fun to read and full of practical wisdom, even if you're an index investor.

Book
📖

Just Keep Buying

Nick Maggiulli's data-driven take on personal finance and investing. More modern than most classics and very grounded in real statistics.

Book

Brokerages We Think Are Worth Your Time

🏛️

Fidelity

Our top pick for beginners. No account minimums, no trading fees, fractional shares, excellent research tools, and real customer service.

Brokerage
🏛️

Charles Schwab

Another excellent long-term brokerage. Strong research, no fees, fractional shares via Schwab Stock Slices, and a well-regarded app experience.

Brokerage
🏛️

Vanguard

Best known for its extremely low-cost index funds. The brokerage interface is dated, but if you just want to own VTI/VOO and leave it alone, Vanguard is fine.
